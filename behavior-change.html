Behavior Change

A project attempting to change peoples unhealthy and infrequent breakfast habits to regular healthier ones.

What did I do? - I worked in a team of 3 conducting design research with diary studies and testing prototypes

What did I use - Sketch

Detailed Project Report

Diary Studies

Participants

We recruited 10 participants - 8 college students, a working professional and a high school study to study their breakfast habits for 4 days.

Design

Participants were instructed to fill out a form and send a picture of their breakfast (if any) over the next 4 days. The form asked questions about their sleeping times, breakfast and acitivties in the morning.

Key Insights from diary study
  • Most people ate breakfast, but more often than not these meals were not healthy or of a sufficient amount
  • Meals tended to be on the go or very quick as the participants had to rush to classes or jobs
  • People often ate whatever was in the cupboard, in the fridge, or lying on the counter - healthy or not
  • People tended to spend more time eating or eating a larger amount if they ate with others

Interview Insights -> Design Choices

  • Wanting to have energy for their daily routines was a component of what and when people ate -> people need a way to conveniently get energy through food in the morning
  • People don’t often think about what they will eat the night before, and leave those thoughts to the busy morning -> designing a system where people don’t have to think about food, instead having it just presented to them is critical
  • Morning routines are very rushed and leave no time for behavior changes like making time to make breakfast themselves -> focusing on convenience and ease of access of the food is important
Key Factors for Design of solution
Time:

Sleep time, wake up time, breakfast prep time, first thing on schedule

Food availibility:

Whats avaiable at home, whats home on the way/at school/work

People

If someone else is cooking, friends are over or going out

Solution

After brainstorming several ideas and considering the above factors we decided to go with a delivery service which would ensure that food is available and no time is spent for the user in its preperation

Design Choices
Delivery vs Pickup

Delivery- with the delivery service, the time a user needs to spend on getting the breakfast is shortened

Raw Materials vs Ready-to-eat

Ready-to-eat- ready-to-eat food cuts the meal preparation time

Subscription vs Order on the Go

Subscription: user doesn’t need to take time out of their busy routine to order everyday

Weekly vs Daily

Daily: daily delivery guarantees that the food is fresh and since its outside their door everyday it serves as a reminder to eat the breakfast

Standardized Menu vs Personalized Menu

Standardized menu: people also spend a lot of time and energy in deciding what they will have, it was also convenient for our prototype to keep the menu standardized

Final Solution

A breakfast subscription service that delivers ready-to-eat breakfast everyday to a specific location at a particular time based on the user’s request. Because convenience/time availability is the #1 reason why our diary study participants are not able to eat healthy breakfast everyday, healthy food is delivered to the user’s location and is delivered every morning to help form a habit

wireflow

Key steps
  • Sign up A user signs up for the service by choosing a plan, specifying food restrictions and when and where he/she wants the food to be delivered
  • Get notificaiions A user will get notified on menus and delivery status
  • Get food A user will receive ready-to-eat food every morning
  • Provide feedback A user can provide feedback on the food and the service

Prototype and field study

We decided to run a field study with a prototype breakfast delivery service. We recruited 5 graduate students who were willing to pay 10$ for 4 days of breakfast delivery. After each day they would fill out a survey asking if they ate the meal and how it fit in their morning routine

  • 2 particpipants had < 2 breakfasts last week
  • 2 particpants had 3-5 breakfasts that werent healthy

participant's breakfast

Results
  • Participants consumed 14/16 of the breakfasts we provided them
  • 2 particpants went from having 1-2 breakfasts to having breakfast on all 4 days
  • 2 participants went from having insufficient/unhealthy breakfast to our alternative
Post study interviews and keytakeaways
  • Sleeping & Breakfast There is a strong connection between sleep and breakfast and we inadvertently also changed some of our participants’ sleeping behaviors where some slept longer because they no longer needed to prepare breakfast and some woke up earlier because they were looking forward to having their breakfast.
  • Breakfast & EnergyHaving the breakfast provides our participants with energy to work during the day. They also get a smaller lunch or get lunch later after having the breakfast because of the energy they get from it
  • Deciding the meal It takes brain power and time to decide what to eat, which is why people didn’t mind having a lot of say in what they were having for breakfast.
  • Broadening the options for breakfast People didn’t consider alternative breakfast options like yogurt, cereal and boiled eggs until they go through the experiment; we changed their perspective on what is possible for breakfast.
