Wanderlust

Wanderlust is an alternative navigation mobile application that encourages serendipity and exploration.

What did I do? - I worked in a team of 5 developers and designers to design the user experience and interface, conduct usability tests and code the frontend of the React Native mobile application. I updated the original project for this portfolio

What did I use - Sketch, Principle, React Native, Adobe Photoshop

Detailed Project Report

I joined the project after it had gone through an initial 10-week design cycle of design research and prototyping.

Recap

Problem

People need a way to find interesting places, avoid unsafe areas, and be immersed in their current environment while navigating. Because traditional maps only suggest the fastest routes, people with the aforementioned desires cannot fulfill their needs

Solution

Wanderlust is an alternative navigation application that provides users with high-level information about their surroundings and lets them wander in a boundary. It aims to facilitate immersion in the environment and worry-free exploration.

Features
  • General directions to a destination without a set route
  • Draw boundary to restrict wandering path
  • Unsafe areas and points of interests are clustered in groups

Iterating by Usability Studies

Iteration #1

We conducted usability studies with the existing application with 3 people visiting the Stanford Campus where we asked them to explore the campus while aiming to get to a destination

Key Insights
  • It was difficult to communicate the idea of an alternative navigation application where users kept expecting the application to tell them which direction to take. They were also confused by the wander boundary and it's purpose
    Fix - An onboarding experience
  • The various colors used to indicate clusters points of interest was extremely confusing as user's found it hard to map colors to points of interests. The initial design hypothesized that clustering points of interests would lead to more immersion but our users were constantly confused about what was around them
    Fix - We removed the clustering of points of interests and separated each out, represented by its own icon on the map. Only unsafe areas were represented by the color red
  • A lot of the confusion during our usability studies were because of dissimilarity between our application and other ones that use maps(uber/lyft/google maps).
    Fix - We worked on a complete redesign of the applicaiton to make it consistent with standards set by other applications
Iteration #2

We conducted another usability study after making the afformentioned fixes

Key Insights
  • Users were still confused about the wander boundary and we received comments such as "I don't know what boundary to draw" We realized that people, especially ones new to an area were not aware of what was around them which is why they couldn't really draw a boundary
    Fix - We still wanted to restrict the users movement so they didnt wander too far from their destination, so we came up with a walkmap to constantly give them a view of how far things were from them
  • Users in our new study almost always found the little compass at the bottom, hard to find and were therefore confused about where to go
    Fix - We removed the compass and changed it to a bouncing arrow to guide the user
